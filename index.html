Evidence & Mathematical Foundation

Final Mathematical Form

Sustainable house = [( Σ(Ni × Ai) ) ÷ ( (P × 1.64) × Or )] + [Ep ÷ (P × 1.64)]

Where:

  • P = Number of people
  • Ep = Solar panel output in kWh/day
  • Or = CO₂ offset rate (e.g., 0.8 for 80%)
  • Ni = Number of plants of type i
  • Ai = CO₂ absorption rate per plant of type i (kg/day)

CO₂ Calculations

Daily CO₂ per person: 1.64 kg/day

Source: Our World in Data - CO₂ emissions per capita

0.6 tonnes/year ÷ 365 days = 0.00164 tonnes/day

0.00164 tonnes × 1000 kg/tonne = 1.64 kg/day

The term ∑(Ni × Ai) / ((P × 1.64) × Or) calculates the overall CO₂ absorption from the plants. This is the fraction of CO₂ absorbed by the plants in relation to the CO₂ that must be offset for the number of people in the household. By multiplying the number of plants Ni by their absorption rate Ai, and dividing by the CO₂ offset requirement, this term ensures that the plant absorption is accurately adjusted to meet the offset needs.

The term Ep / (P × 1.64) estimates the contribution of solar energy based on per capita daily energy needs, adjusting it for the number of people and the energy output per person (which is 1.64 kWh/day as per your calculation). This ensures that the solar panel output is appropriately accounted for based on the household's energy needs.

Formula in Mathematical Form

You'll See Equations Like:

CO₂ Absorption Needed

People × 1.64 × Offset Rate

Example Calculation:

For 4 people with 50% offset target:

4 × 1.64 × 0.5 = 3.28 kg CO₂/day needed

Panels Needed

Electricity / Panel Output

Example Calculation:

For 6.56 kWh/day (4 people):

6.56 ÷ 1.5 = 4.37 (5 panels needed)

Total Plant Absorption

∑ (Plant Count × Absorption Rate)

Example Calculation:

2 Peepal (0.18) + 3 Snake (0.006)

= 0.186 kg CO₂/day total

Important Notes on Calculations

  • All calculations use daily rates for consistency
  • Panel output assumes 5 hours peak sunlight
  • Plant absorption varies by climate and care
